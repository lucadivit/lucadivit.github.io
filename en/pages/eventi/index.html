Events & Talks

My Talks

  • All Aboard Neural ODEs!

    Neural networks with discrete layers are nice, right? But what happens when layers become infinite? In this talk we hop in for a ride that starts with derivatives and integrals, passes through differential equations and numerical methods, and lands on Neural ODEs. We will explore what it means to have a model that evolves continuously over time instead of growing layer by layer, look at strengths and trade-offs, and close with a small practical example.

  • Backprop? Not Required

    Who said a neural network must be trained with backpropagation? In this lightning talk, we will see how a metaheuristic algorithm can learn to do the job of classical gradient-based optimization, finding neural network weights without ever computing a derivative. A curious alternative approach, sitting at the crossroads of evolution, optimization, and a small rebellion against backprop dogma.

  • Fantastic Ciphers and How to Break Them

    Cryptography is one of the pillars of modern security, but what happens when the ciphers protecting our information are put to the test? In this talk, we will explore the mathematical foundations that make famous ciphers secure, starting from simple ones like Caesar and moving to widely used systems such as RSA and ElGamal. With a pinch of number theory and a bit of logic, we will show how cryptography can be attacked and why vulnerabilities are always around the corner, ready to be exploited. Get ready to discover the beauty of numbers and the power of mathematics to crack systems we often assume are invulnerable. A fascinating journey through ciphers, codes, and hacks.

  • What If Machines Evolved Like Humans?

    Neuroevolution is an alternative to classic backpropagation for training neural networks. When used properly, it brings advantages over common neural network design approaches: reduced overfitting and underfitting risk, fewer vanishing and exploding gradient issues, incremental construction, and fully automatic optimization of network architecture. This opens new frontiers in Deep Learning, bringing machines closer to humans not only in solving complex tasks, but also in how they learn through spontaneous evolutionary mechanisms.

  • To Err Is Human, Machines Learn

    Reinforcement learning is one of the most fascinating paradigms in machine learning and is drawing increasing interest from the scientific community, which continues to broaden its application areas. In this talk, we cover the core concepts of this paradigm, explain Q-Learning as a practical algorithm, and show its application on a Maze game case study.

  • GNNs: The Great Unknown

    Graphs are both ancient and fundamental tools for modeling complex networks across many domains, from biology to social network analysis. At the core of this revolution lies the family of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), a class of algorithms that can learn directly from graph structures. In this talk, we take a tour of the graph learning landscape. We will explore the power of DeepWalk, a graph representation learning algorithm, and then move to GCNs, true milestones in the evolution of GNNs, uncovering how they work and where they shine.

  • Stop Chasing Objectives, Start Innovating

    In an increasingly competitive world, we have stopped acting in favor of simply getting things done. Education, professionalism, and similar pressures push us toward targets and outcomes, making us forget why we chose a path in the first place, and why walking it can be meaningful. Real creativity is not in chasing objectives, but in innovating: doing something never done before because that can become the stepping stone to something bigger. Novelty Search is a goal-free evolutionary process that can create neural networks able to solve tasks without being explicitly trained for them. Between philosophy and algorithmic pragmatism, it teaches us how the will to innovate without fixed objectives can still lead to greatness. Greatness Cannot Be Planned - K.O.S.

  • Evolution Through Genetic Algorithms

    Genetic algorithms are a class of artificial intelligence methods, specifically in evolutionary computation. They are used to tackle optimization problems for which no linear or polynomial-time solutions are known.

  • Python Beginner's Workshop

    An introductory workshop designed for complete beginners, focused on Python fundamentals. During the session, we explored the core language concepts and then put them into practice by building a simple interactive rock-paper-scissors game.

  • The Sound Of Malware

    Hello CVEs, my old friends, I have come to deal with you again... Because a backdoor softly creeping... Oops, sorry, we were singing out loud. It is what we keep doing whenever a new vulnerability breaks into our daily routine. Recently this has happened more and more often because of software dependencies. Log4J, you said? Oh well, do not forget XZUtils. They call these supply chain attacks. We started addressing them with SLSA (read "salsa"), then realized we could do more. Much more. So we transformed malicious binaries into... waveforms. And we started making them groove. By reading binaries as if they were regular waveforms and analyzing them with a bit of math (cepstrum, Fourier series, and friends), we built a model aimed at detecting whether a dependency is malicious, and even classifying it by malware family. Fascinating, right? The sound of malware...

  • Q&A For Dummies

    In this talk, we explored how to build an intelligent FAQ system using language models, embeddings, and vector search. With Python, we saw how to represent content semantically and retrieve the most relevant answers based on user questions.
